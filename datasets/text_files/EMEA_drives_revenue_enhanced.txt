## Simple explanations of technical terms

- **Revenue**: The money a company earns from selling its products or services.
- **Revenue surge**: A big jump (a sudden increase) in revenue.
- **Quarter (Q2)**: A quarter is **3 months** of the year. **Q2** means the second 3-month period (usually April to June).
- **Sales volume**: How much the company sold (the amount of deals or products sold).
- **Sales activity**: The work and actions that lead to sales (calls, meetings, negotiations, closing deals).
- **Year-over-Year (YoY)**: Comparing this period to the **same period last year** (for example, Q2 this year vs. Q2 last year).
- **2.3x**: “x” means “times.” **2.3x** means **2.3 times bigger** than before.
- **Top contributors**: The places that added the most to the total sales.
- **Execution**: How well the team did their plan (did they actually do what they intended?).
- **Market penetration**: How well the company is entering a market—basically, **how many customers in that area are buying from them**.
- **Growth trajectory**: The direction and speed of growth over time (growing fast, slowly, or not much).
- **Stable quarter**: Not much change—sales stayed roughly similar to before.
- **Region**: A large area of the world grouped together (like EMEA, Americas, APAC).
