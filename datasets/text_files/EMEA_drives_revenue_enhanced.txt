**Europe, Middle East, and Africa Drives Revenue Surge in Quarter 2 of 2019: Business Development Team Delivers Strong Year-over-Year Growth**

In **Quarter 2 of 2019 (Q2 2019)**, the **Business Development (BD)** team helped the company sell a lot more than before. **Europe, the Middle East, and Africa (EMEA)** was the biggest reason the sales went up. Compared with the same time last year, sales became as much as **2.3 times bigger**.

---

## Key Highlights (in simpler words)

### **Europe, Middle East, and Africa (EMEA) leads**
Sales in **20 countries** across **Europe, the Middle East, and Africa** pushed the strong results in **Quarter 2 of 2019**.

### **Top countries that helped the most**
These places made up the biggest parts of total sales in **Quarter 2 of 2019**:
- **United States (US): 28%**
- **Spain: 20%**
- **India: 15%**
- **Norway: 11%**

*(Percent means “out of 100.” For example, 28% means 28 out of every 100 sales came from the United States.)*

### **Year-over-Year growth**
The **Business Development team** increased sales compared to the same quarter last year (**Year-over-Year, or YoY**). This shows they did a good job finding customers and growing in the market.

---

## Breakdown by Region (what each big area did)

### **Europe, Middle East, and Africa (EMEA)**
The best-performing region. It made up a large part of total sales because sales activity was strong in important countries.

### **Americas**
This region (mostly North and South America) added a good amount of sales, but it grew more slowly than **EMEA**.

### **Asia-Pacific (APAC)**
**Asia-Pacific (APAC)** means countries in Asia and the Pacific area (like Japan, Australia, and others). This region stayed **pretty steady**, meaning sales didn’t change a lot this quarter.

## Simple explanations of technical terms

- **Revenue**: The money a company earns from selling things.
- **Sales volume**: How much the company sold (could mean number of deals or amount sold).
- **Quarter (Q2)**: A 3-month part of the year. **Q2** is usually April–June.
- **Year-over-Year (YoY)**: Comparing something to the same time one year earlier.
- **2.3x**: “2.3 times.” If you had 10 before, 2.3x would be 23.
- **Market penetration**: How well the company is reaching and selling to people in a market (like a country or region).
- **Growth engine**: The main part that is pushing growth the most.
- **Execution**: How well the team carried out their plans.
- **Trajectory**: The direction things are going (growing fast, slowly, or staying flat).
- **Stable**: Not changing much.
